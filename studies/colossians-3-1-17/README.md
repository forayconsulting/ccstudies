# Colossians 3:1-17 — Unified Application

## From Identity to Transformation: Seeking, Putting Off, and Putting On

A synthesized application of Colossians 3:1-17, drawing on the exegetical findings from the three section studies (3:1-4, 3:5-11, 3:12-17), using the methodology from *Hermeneutics and Exegesis for Laypeople* by Clayton Chancey.

---

## Purpose

The individual studies of Colossians 3:1-4, 3:5-11, and 3:12-17 each worked through the full exegetical process: asking the text questions, outlining, and consulting exegetical resources. Each study also answered the four application questions for its own section.

This document brings the full passage together for a unified application. Paul wrote these seventeen verses as a single, connected argument — three movements of one symphony. The application document treats them accordingly, tracing the argument's flow from heavenly orientation (vv. 1-4) through the decisive removal of the old self's vices (vv. 5-11) to the deliberate clothing of the new self in Christlike character and corporate worship (vv. 12-17).

## Key Themes Across the Full Passage

- **The indicative-imperative structure**: every command is grounded in what God has already done in Christ
- **The sufficiency of Christ** against the Colossian heresy and its modern equivalents
- **The clothing metaphor**: stripping off the old self's garments (vv. 5-11) and putting on the new self's wardrobe (vv. 12-17)
- **Sanctification as image-restoration**: God is renewing believers according to His original design (v. 10)
- **Thanksgiving as the antithesis of greed**: gratitude (vv. 15-17) counters the idolatrous acquisitiveness condemned in v. 5
- **Christ governs every dimension**: peace of Christ (v. 15), word of Christ (v. 16), name of the Lord Jesus (v. 17)
