# Colossians 3:5-11 — Outline

## Genre Identification

Colossians is an **epistle** (personal letter). The quintessential unit of thought in epistolary literature is a **sentence**. Each complete sentence is a unit to be summarized and synthesized.

---

## Book-Level Outline of Colossians

Before outlining 3:5-11, we need to understand where this passage sits within the whole letter. The following outline traces the major movements of the book.

    (1:1-2) Greeting

    (1:3-2:5) Thanksgiving and the Supremacy of Christ

        (1:3-8) Thanksgiving for the Colossians' Faith
        (1:9-14) Prayer for Spiritual Growth
        (1:15-20) The Supremacy of Christ over All Creation
        (1:21-23) Reconciliation through Christ
        (1:24-2:5) Paul's Ministry of the Mystery of Christ

    (2:6-23) Warning against False Teaching

        (2:6-7) Continue in Christ as You Received Him
        (2:8-15) The Sufficiency of Christ vs. Human Philosophy
        (2:16-23) Freedom from Human Regulations

    (3:1-4:6) The New Life in Christ

        (3:1-4) Seek the Things Above
        (3:5-11) Put to Death the Old Self
        (3:12-17) Put On the New Self
        (3:18-4:1) Household Instructions
        (4:2-6) Exhortation to Prayer and Wisdom

    (4:7-18) Final Greetings and Instructions

Our passage (3:5-11) falls within the practical section of the letter ("The New Life in Christ"), directly after the theological foundation of the believer's position in Christ (3:1-4) and directly before the positive virtues of the new self (3:12-17). The "therefore" of 3:5 connects the ethical commands to the doctrinal foundation of chapters 1-2 and 3:1-4.

## Sentence-Level Outline of Colossians 3:5-11

### Step 1: Divide into Sentence Units

| Unit | Reference | Text (NASB) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3:5 | "Therefore consider the members of your earthly body as dead to immorality, impurity, passion, evil desire, and greed, which amounts to idolatry." |
| 2 | 3:6 | "For it is because of these things that the wrath of God will come upon the sons of disobedience," |
| 3 | 3:7 | "and in them you also once walked, when you were living in them." |
| 4 | 3:8 | "But now you also, put them all aside: anger, wrath, malice, slander, and abusive speech from your mouth." |
| 5 | 3:9a | "Do not lie to one another," |
| 6 | 3:9b-10 | "since you laid aside the old self with its evil practices, and have put on the new self who is being renewed to a true knowledge according to the image of the One who created him —" |
| 7 | 3:11 | "a renewal in which there is no distinction between Greek and Jew, circumcised and uncircumcised, barbarian, Scythian, slave and freeman, but Christ is all, and in all." |

Note: Verses 6-7 function as a single grammatical unit in Greek (one sentence), but they contain two distinct thoughts: the reason for the command (v. 6) and the Colossians' former participation in those sins (v. 7). I have separated them for clarity. Similarly, v. 9a contains the final imperative, while 9b-10 provides its theological basis.

### Step 2: Summarize Each Unit

    (3:5)     Put to death the sins of desire, for greed is idolatry
    (3:6)     For God's wrath will come because of these things
    (3:7)     You also once lived this way
    (3:8)     But now put aside the sins of speech and hostility
    (3:9a)    Do not lie to one another
    (3:9b-10) Since you have put off the old self and put on the new self, being renewed in God's image
    (3:11)    A renewal in which all human distinctions are abolished; Christ is all, and in all

### Step 3: Group by Commonality (Synthesis)

Three movements emerge:

1. **Vv. 5-7**: The first command — "put to death" — targeting sins of internal desire, supported by the motivation of divine wrath and the reminder of their former life.
2. **Vv. 8-9a**: The second command — "put aside" — targeting sins of social interaction and speech.
3. **Vv. 9b-11**: The theological basis — the old self has been removed, the new self has been put on and is being renewed in God's image, resulting in a new unified humanity.

### Step 4: Title Each Movement

    (3:5-7) Put to Death the Sins of Desire
    (3:8-9a) Put Aside the Sins of Speech
    (3:9b-11) The Basis: The Old Self Removed, the New Self Renewed

### Step 5: Synthesize into a Single Header

    (3:5-11) Putting Off the Old Self: From Death to Renewal

## Key Conjunctions and Logical Movement

| Verse | Conjunction | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3:5 | **Therefore** (Οὖν) | Inferential | Connects the ethical command to the theological foundation of 3:1-4. "Because you have died and been raised with Christ, therefore put to death..." |
| 3:6 | **For** (δι' ἅ) | Inferential | Provides the reason/motivation for the command in v. 5: God's wrath is coming because of these sins. |
| 3:7 | **and...also** (καί) | Coordinating | Adds a second motivation: you yourselves used to participate in these sins. |
| 3:8 | **But now** (νυνὶ δέ) | Adversative | Marks a sharp contrast between the Colossians' past ("once walked," v. 7) and their present obligation ("but now... put them aside"). This is the pivot of the passage. |
| 3:9 | **since** (ἀπεκδυσάμενοι) | Subordinating (participial) | Provides the theological ground for the commands: you have already put off the old self. The imperative is grounded in the indicative. |
| 3:10 | **and** (καί) | Coordinating | Links the removal of the old self with the assumption of the new self — they are two sides of one act. |
| 3:11 | **where** (ὅπου) | Subordinating | Introduces the sphere of the renewal: in this new reality, human distinctions are abolished. |

## Observations from the Outline

1. **Indicative grounds imperative.** The two commands ("put to death," v. 5; "put aside," v. 8) are grounded in the theological reality of vv. 9b-10. Paul does not simply tell the Colossians to try harder — he reminds them of what has already happened in Christ.

2. **Two categories of sin.** The first vice list (v. 5) addresses **internal sins of desire** (immorality, impurity, passion, evil desire, greed). The second vice list (v. 8) addresses **external sins of hostility and speech** (anger, wrath, malice, slander, abusive speech, lying). The progression moves from the heart outward to relationships.

3. **The climax is v. 11.** The passage moves from command (vv. 5, 8) through basis (vv. 9-10) to climax (v. 11). The pinnacle is not the vice lists but the declaration that "Christ is all, and in all." The ethical transformation Paul demands is not an end in itself — it serves the creation of a new, unified humanity centered on Christ.

4. **"But now" (v. 8) is the structural pivot.** The adversative conjunction "but now" divides the passage between the Colossians' past (vv. 5-7, "you once walked") and their present obligation (vv. 8-11, "but now put aside"). This mirrors the larger structure of Colossians, where Paul regularly contrasts the believers' former condition with their present status in Christ (cf. 1:21-22).
