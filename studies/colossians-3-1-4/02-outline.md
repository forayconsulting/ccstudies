# Step 2: Outline the Text — Colossians 3:1-4

## Genre and Unit of Thought

Colossians is an **epistle** (letter). The unit of thought for epistolary literature is the **sentence**. Each complete sentence conveys a single, complete thought and serves as a building block for the author's argument.

## Text (NASB 1995)

> *(1) Therefore, if you have been raised with Christ, keep seeking the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. (2) Set your minds on the things that are above, not on the things that are on earth. (3) For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. (4) When Christ, who is our life, is revealed, then you too will be revealed with Him in glory.*

---

## Broader Context: Where 3:1-4 Sits in Colossians

Before outlining our passage, it helps to identify where it falls in the letter's major divisions:

>     (1:1-2) Greeting
>
>     (1:3-2:5) Doctrinal Foundation — The Supremacy and Sufficiency of Christ
>
>     (2:6-23) Polemical Section — Refutation of the False Teaching
>
>     **(3:1-4:6) Practical Section — Life in Christ**
>
>     (4:7-18) Closing Greetings

Colossians 3:1-4 is the **hinge** between the polemical section (chapter 2) and the practical section (3:5 and following). Paul transitions from arguing *against* the false teaching to arguing *for* a Christ-centered life. The inferential "Therefore" in 3:1 makes this transition explicit.

---

## Sentence-Level Breakdown

### Unit 1 — Verse 1

> *"Therefore, if you have been raised with Christ, keep seeking the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God."*

**Conjunctions/Logical Markers:**

| Word | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Therefore** (οὖν) | Inferential | Draws a conclusion from chapters 1-2 — specifically from 2:20-23, where Paul established that believers died with Christ to the elementary principles |
| **if** (εἰ) | Subordinating (conditional) | First-class condition in Greek — assumes the condition is true. Better rendered "since" or "given that." Paul is not questioning whether they have been raised; he is affirming it as the basis for his command |

**Structure:** Conditional clause (protasis) + imperative (apodosis)
- *Protasis:* "If you have been raised with Christ" — the indicative reality (what is true)
- *Apodosis:* "keep seeking the things that are above" — the imperative command (what to do)
- *Qualifier:* "where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God" — defines "things above" by Christ's location and position

**Summary:** *Since you have been raised with Christ, keep seeking heavenly things where Christ reigns.*

---

### Unit 2 — Verse 2

> *"Set your minds on the things that are above, not on the things that are on earth."*

**Conjunctions/Logical Markers:**

| Word | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|
| *(asyndeton)* | — | No conjunction connects this sentence to v1. This creates a sharp, emphatic effect — Paul issues a second command without transition, reinforcing the first |
| **not** (μή) | Adversative (negation) | Creates an explicit contrast between "things above" and "things on earth" |

**Structure:** Imperative + contrast
- *Command:* "Set your minds on the things that are above"
- *Contrast:* "not on the things that are on earth"

**Relationship to Unit 1:** This sentence **intensifies and sharpens** the command in v1. "Keep seeking" (ζητεῖτε) in v1 involves active pursuit; "set your minds" (φρονεῖτε) in v2 involves the orientation of one's thinking and disposition. Together, they cover both action and mindset.

**Summary:** *Orient your thinking toward heavenly things, not earthly things.*

---

### Unit 3 — Verse 3

> *"For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God."*

**Conjunctions/Logical Markers:**

| Word | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **For** (γάρ) | Inferential/Causal | Provides the *reason* for the commands in vv1-2. Paul now explains *why* they should seek things above and set their minds above |
| **and** (καί) | Coordinating | Links two parallel indicative statements about the believer's identity |

**Structure:** Causal clause with two coordinated statements
- *Statement 1:* "You have died" — past, completed action (aorist tense). The old life terminated at the cross.
- *Statement 2:* "Your life is hidden with Christ in God" — present, ongoing reality (perfect tense). The new life is currently secure.

**Relationship to Units 1-2:** This is the **theological ground** for the imperatives. The commands to seek and think about things above are not arbitrary — they flow from the reality that the believer's old earthly life has ended and their true life is now in the heavenly realm with Christ.

**Summary:** *Because you died and your true life is now securely hidden in Christ.*

---

### Unit 4 — Verse 4

> *"When Christ, who is our life, is revealed, then you too will be revealed with Him in glory."*

**Conjunctions/Logical Markers:**

| Word | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **When** (ὅταν) | Subordinating (temporal) | Introduces a temporal clause pointing to a future event — the return of Christ |
| **then... too** (τότε καί) | Inferential/Coordinating | Draws the consequence: when Christ appears, believers will appear with Him |

**Structure:** Temporal protasis + consequent apodosis
- *Temporal clause:* "When Christ, who is our life, is revealed" — the future event
- *Parenthetical identification:* "who is our life" — defines Christ's relationship to believers
- *Consequence:* "then you too will be revealed with Him in glory" — the believer's destiny

**Relationship to Unit 3:** This sentence resolves the tension created in v3. The believer's life is currently *hidden* — but it will not always be so. What is now concealed will one day be *revealed*. The hiddenness is temporary; the glory is the destination.

**Summary:** *When Christ appears, you will appear with Him in glory.*

---

## Synthesis: Grouping the Units

Working from the summaries:

>     (3:1) *Since you have been raised with Christ, keep seeking heavenly things where Christ reigns*
>
>     (3:2) *Orient your thinking toward heavenly things, not earthly things*
>
>     (3:3) *Because you died and your true life is now securely hidden in Christ*
>
>     (3:4) *When Christ appears, you will appear with Him in glory*

Two natural groupings emerge based on grammatical mood:

- **Verses 1-2** are **imperative** — they issue commands (what to do)
- **Verses 3-4** are **indicative** — they state realities (what is true)

Furthermore, vv3-4 provide the *ground* for vv1-2 (signaled by "For" in v3). The structure is:

1. **Command** (vv1-2): Seek and think about things above
2. **Reason** (vv3-4): Because of who you are and what you will become

Within each pair, there is also internal movement:

- v1 → v2: From *action* (seeking) to *mindset* (thinking) — intensification
- v3 → v4: From *past/present reality* (you died; your life is hidden) to *future hope* (you will be revealed in glory) — temporal progression

## Observations from the Outline

1. **Imperative follows indicative.** Paul's ethical commands are always rooted in theological realities. He does not say "do this to become something" but "because you are this, do this." The order matters.

2. **"Therefore" anchors the passage to the preceding argument.** This is not a standalone devotional thought. It is the logical conclusion of Paul's extended argument about the supremacy and sufficiency of Christ (ch. 1) and the refutation of the false teaching (ch. 2). Isolating 3:1-4 from its literary context would obscure Paul's intended meaning.

3. **The passage has a chiastic shape:**
   - A — Raised with Christ (v1a) — *past union*
   - B — Seek/set your mind above (vv1b-2) — *present command*
   - B' — You have died; life hidden (v3) — *present reality*
   - A' — Revealed with Christ in glory (v4) — *future union*

   The outer frame (A/A') speaks of the believer's union with Christ in resurrection — past and future. The inner frame (B/B') addresses the present life — what to do and why. Christ brackets everything.

4. **"Things above" is defined personally, not spatially.** Paul does not describe heaven as a location to daydream about. "Things above" are defined as "where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God" (v1). The upward orientation is toward a *Person*, not a *place*.

5. **The hiddenness is purposeful.** The believer's life is not hidden *from* God but hidden *with Christ in* God. This is protective concealment, not abandonment. The world cannot see the believer's true identity — but God holds it secure until the day of revelation.
