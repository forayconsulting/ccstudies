# Step 3: Use Exegetical Resources — Colossians 3:1-4

This file documents findings from exegetical tools applied to Colossians 3:1-4. Following the methodology, these resources were consulted *after* forming initial observations through asking the text questions (Step 1) and outlining (Step 2). The goal is to refine, confirm, or correct the conclusions reached through personal study — not to outsource interpretation.

## Word Studies

> **Warning:** Context is king. Just because a word *can* mean something doesn't mean it *does* mean that in every occurrence. The following definitions are evaluated in light of their Colossians 3 context.

### συνηγέρθητε (*synēgerthēte*) — "you have been raised with" (v1)

- **Form:** Aorist passive indicative, 2nd person plural of συνεγείρω (*synegeirō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To raise together with, to cause to come back to life together with" (BDAG)
- **Contextual meaning:** The aorist tense points to a completed past event. The passive voice indicates God as the agent — the Colossians did not raise themselves. The prefix συν- ("with") is critical: this is co-resurrection *with Christ*. Paul uses this same compound verb in Ephesians 2:6 and Colossians 2:12.
- **Significance:** This is not future physical resurrection (that comes in v4). This is a spiritual reality that has already occurred. Believers have been transferred from death to life in union with Christ's resurrection. The false teachers offered additional spiritual experiences; Paul says the decisive spiritual event has already happened.

### ζητεῖτε (*zēteite*) — "keep seeking" (v1)

- **Form:** Present active imperative, 2nd person plural of ζητέω (*zēteō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To seek, look for, investigate, strive for" (Abbott-Smith); "to devote serious effort to realize one's desire or objective" (BDAG)
- **Contextual meaning:** The present tense imperative indicates *continuous* action — not a one-time decision but an ongoing pursuit. This is a lifestyle command. The object ("things above") is not abstract — it is defined immediately by Christ's location.
- **Contrast with the false teaching:** The false teachers told the Colossians to seek spiritual fullness through rituals, asceticism, and mystical experiences. Paul redirects: seek Christ.

### φρονεῖτε (*phroneite*) — "set your minds on" (v2)

- **Form:** Present active imperative, 2nd person plural of φρονέω (*phroneō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To think, to have a mindset, to set one's mind on, to be intent on" (BDAG); "to exercise the mind, to interest oneself in with concern or obedience" (Abbott-Smith)
- **Contextual meaning:** Broader than mere intellectual thought. φρονέω encompasses one's entire mental and moral disposition — how one thinks, what one values, what occupies one's attention. Paul uses the same word in Philippians 2:5 ("Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus") and Romans 8:5 ("Those who are according to the flesh set their minds on the things of the flesh").
- **Relationship to ζητεῖτε (v1):** If "seeking" is the external pursuit, "setting the mind" is the internal orientation. Together they encompass the totality of the believer's life — both outward activity and inward disposition directed toward Christ.

### ἀπεθάνετε (*apethanete*) — "you have died" (v3)

- **Form:** Aorist active indicative, 2nd person plural of ἀποθνῄσκω (*apothnēskō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To die, to cease to exist in terms of some status or situation" (BDAG)
- **Contextual meaning:** Paul stated the same reality in 2:20 — "If you have died with Christ to the elementary principles of the world, why, as if you were living in the world, do you submit yourselves to decrees...?" The death is a past, completed event (aorist). It refers not to physical death but to the believer's co-crucifixion with Christ (Rom 6:6; Gal 2:20). The old self — with its bondage to worldly systems, including the very regulations the false teachers promoted — has been put to death.

### κέκρυπται (*kekryptai*) — "is hidden" (v3)

- **Form:** Perfect passive indicative, 3rd person singular of κρύπτω (*kryptō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To hide, to keep secret, to conceal" (Abbott-Smith); "to keep from being seen, to store up" (BDAG)
- **Contextual meaning:** The perfect tense is significant — it describes a past action with ongoing results. The believer's life *was* hidden at conversion and *remains* hidden now. The passive voice suggests God as the agent of concealment. "Hidden with Christ in God" describes a double enclosure: the believer's life is in Christ, and Christ is in God. This is not hiddenness as obscurity but as *security* and *protection*.
- **Theological note:** The English word "cryptic" derives from this Greek root. The believer's true identity is not visible to the world — it is a mystery concealed in God's safekeeping, awaiting future revelation (v4).

### φανερωθῇ (*phanerōthē*) — "is revealed" (v4)

- **Form:** Aorist passive subjunctive, 3rd person singular of φανερόω (*phaneroō*)
- **Lexical range:** "To make known, to make visible, to reveal, to cause to become known" (BDAG)
- **Contextual meaning:** The word creates a deliberate contrast with κέκρυπται in v3. What is now *hidden* will then be *revealed*. The passive voice again points to God as the agent — God will reveal Christ at the appointed time. The subjunctive mood with ὅταν ("when") expresses a temporal condition: the timing is certain in God's plan, even if unknown to us.
- **Eschatological significance:** φανερόω is used elsewhere for Christ's incarnation (1 Tim 3:16; 1 John 1:2) and second coming (1 Pet 5:4; 1 John 2:28; 3:2). The same God who revealed Christ in the incarnation will reveal Him again in glory.

## Contextual Observations from Colossians 2

Understanding the immediate context is essential since 3:1 begins with "Therefore." Paul's argument in chapter 2 directly shapes the meaning of 3:1-4:

- **2:8** — The false teaching is "philosophy and empty deception, according to the tradition of men, according to the elementary principles of the world, rather than according to Christ." The contrast: human tradition vs. Christ.
- **2:9-10** — "In Him all the fullness of Deity dwells in bodily form, and in Him you have been made complete." The Colossians don't need anything beyond Christ — they are already complete.
- **2:11-13** — Believers have experienced a spiritual circumcision, burial, and resurrection with Christ. This is the foundation for 3:1 ("if you have been raised with Christ").
- **2:14-15** — Christ's cross cancelled the debt and disarmed spiritual powers. The basis for the believer's freedom from worldly regulations.
- **2:16-19** — Therefore, no one should act as judge regarding food, drink, festivals, or angel worship. These are "a shadow of what is to come; but the substance belongs to Christ."
- **2:20-23** — "If you died with Christ to the elementary principles of the world, why do you submit to decrees?" Ascetic regulations have "the appearance of wisdom" but are "of no value against fleshly indulgence." This is the negative side; 3:1-4 provides the positive alternative.

The movement: You are complete in Christ (2:9-10) → You have died and been raised with Christ (2:11-13) → Christ has triumphed (2:14-15) → So reject the false teaching (2:16-23) → **Therefore, seek the things above where Christ is (3:1-4).**

## Summary of Exegetical Findings

1. **The passage's meaning is controlled by its literary context.** The "Therefore" of 3:1 cannot be interpreted apart from chapters 1-2. Paul's positive commands flow directly from his refutation of the Colossian heresy and his assertion of Christ's supremacy and sufficiency.

2. **The Greek tenses are theologically significant.** The aorist verbs ("you were raised," "you died") describe completed past events. The perfect verb ("is hidden") describes an ongoing state resulting from a past event. The present imperatives ("keep seeking," "set your minds") demand continuous action. The future "will be revealed" provides the eschatological horizon. Together, they trace the believer's life from past salvation through present obedience to future glorification.

3. **The two imperatives (ζητεῖτε, φρονεῖτε) are comprehensive.** They address both external activity and internal disposition. Paul is calling for a total reorientation of life around the exalted Christ.

4. **"Things above" is not platonic dualism.** Paul is not condemning the material world or promoting an escapist spirituality. "Things on earth" in context refers to the earthly religious system the false teachers promoted (cf. 2:20-23). "Things above" is defined personally — "where Christ is" — not as an abstract spiritual realm.

5. **The "hidden/revealed" contrast resolves the present tension.** Believers' true identity is not yet visible to the world. This explains why the Christian life can seem unimpressive by worldly standards. But the hiddenness is temporary and protective. The revelation is certain and glorious.

6. **"Christ, who is our life" is the theological apex.** Christ is not merely the source or sustainer of the believer's life — He *is* that life. This identification is the deepest expression of union with Christ in this passage and undercuts every system that would supplement Christ with additional requirements for spiritual vitality.
