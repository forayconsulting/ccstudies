# Glossary

**Interpretation** - *A judgment as to the meaning of a text.*

**Hermeneutics** - *The principles used to determine an interpretation of a text.*

**Exegesis** - *The process used to determine an interpretation of a text.*

**Authorial Intent** - *The original meaning intended by the author of a text.*

**Verbal Plenary Inspiration** - *The precise wording of Scripture is God-breathed, and therefore bears Gods authority.*

**Dictation Theory of Inspiration** - *God bypassed the intentions and circumstances of the human authors, "dictating" the entire content of the Bible.*

**Partial/Conceptual Inspiration** - *God impressed concepts into the minds of the human authors, and they wrote about them in detailed by potentially flawed ways.*

**Natural Theory of Inspiration** - *There is no divinity in the inspiration of the Bible. The intentions and circumstances of the human authors are all there is, and therefore, they have no real authority.*

**Literal Historical Grammatic Hermeneutic** - *A commonsense reading, taking into account the historical setting and linguistic choices of the author to determine his originally intended meaning.*

**Moral/Allegorical Hermeneutic** - *A mystical or codical reading, often attempting to identify secret patterns of words, numbers, or locations in order to unpack a more significant meaning.*

**Theological Hermeneutic** - *A theology-driven reading, interpreting individual passages of Scripture in the light of theological presuppositions.*

**Metanarrative** - *The overarching story comprised of and connecting smaller stories.*

**Outlining** - *The process of notating the structure, themes, and argument flow of a book of the Bible.*

**Summary** - *Condensing a lengthy, difficult, or complicated passage into a simple statement.*

**Synthesis** - *Combining multiple sources, ideas, or passages to form a single argument.*
