# Glossary

> **Anagogical Hermeneutic** – A mystical or codical reading, often attempting to identify secret patterns of words, numbers, or locations in order to unpack a more significant meaning.

> **Authorial Intent** – The original meaning intended by the author of a text.

> **Dual Authorship** – The concept that every passage of Scripture has both a human and a divine author.

> **Exegesis** – The process used to determine the interpretation of a text.

> **Hermeneutics** – The principles used to determine an interpretation of a text.

> **Inspiration** – The means by which God communicated His words to mankind.

> **Interpretation** – A judgment as to the meaning of a text.

> **Literal Historical Grammatical Hermeneutic** – A commonsense reading, taking into account the historical setting and linguistic choices of the author to determine his originally intended meaning.

> **Metanarrative** – The overarching story comprised of and connecting smaller stories.

> **Moral/Allegorical Hermeneutic** – An application-driven reading, prioritizing the personal relevance of the text over the actual meaning of the text.

> **Outlining** – The process of notating the structure, themes, and argument flow of a book of the Bible.

> **Summary** – Condensing a lengthy, difficult, or complicated passage into a simple statement.

> **Synthesis** – Combining multiple sources, ideas, or passages to form a single argument.

> **Theological Hermeneutic** – A theology-driven reading, interpreting individual passages of Scripture in the light of theological presuppositions.

> **Verbal Plenary Inspiration** – The view that the precise wording of Scripture is God-breathed and thus bears God's authority.
